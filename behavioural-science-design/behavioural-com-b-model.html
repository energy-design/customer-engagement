The COM-B Model

The COM-B Model is a psychological framework that identifies the key components necessary for behaviour change. Developed by Susan Michie and colleagues, it stands for Capability, Opportunity, Motivation, and Behaviour, and it emphasises the interconnections between these elements.

The model posits that for a behaviour to occur, an individual must have the Capability, the Opportunity, and the Motivation to perform it.

How does it work?

The COM-B Model is made of the three components which are involved in any behaviour. These are:

  • Capability (C): One must believe they have the physical and psychological capability of changing their behaviour
  • Opportunity (O): This refers to the external factors that make the desire behaviour change possible
  • Motivation (M): The internal processes which influence our decision making and behaviours

The components of the COM-B Model intact as follows:

  • C and O often interact to influence M
  • C, O, M influence Behaviour (B) together
  • Behaviour influences these three components too which creates a feedback loop that leads to long term behaviour

Successful interventions will change one or more of these components in a way that maximises the new behaviour and reduces risk of old behaviours being repeated.

Capability

  • Physical: Physical skill, strength or stamina to enact the target behaviour
  • Psychological: Knowledge or psychological skills to engage in the necessary mental processes

Opportunity

  • Physical: Opportunity provided by the environment involving time, resources, locations and cues
  • Social: Opportunity afforded by interpersonal influences, social cues and cultural norms

Motivation

  • Automatic: Processes involving emotional reactions, desires, impulses, drive states and reflex responses
  • Reflective: Processes involving plans (self-conscious intentions) and evaluations (what is good / bad)

The value of using this model

Strengths

  • Developed to tackle broader behaviour change challenges with complex multiple variables
  • Enables us to identify biggest behavioural barriers and then provides actionable techniques to overcome such barriers
  • Gaps in Capability, Opportunity and Motivation can be considered at each stage of the customer journey and so can take into account the changing context
  • Readiness to change can be incorporated within psychological capability and reflective motivation.

Limitations

  • Complex behaviour change system with many different layers
  • Limited prioritisation within the 93 identified Behaviour Change Techniques
  • Has not been validated in an employment context
  • Categorisation into COM-B components can be subjective
