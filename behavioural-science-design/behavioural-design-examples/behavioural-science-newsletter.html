Our vision

British Gas has always had a email newsletter as a way to engage customers in-life, across retention, upselling and brand awareness. From this experience, we knew a newsletter would be a good format for creating a consistent ‘hum’ to support business objectives as well as providing customers with tips and advice to nurture their relationship with British Gas.

Our goal was to cultivate lasting relationships with our customers by providing them with meaningful rewards and personalised experiences. Additionally, we were eager to keep our customers informed about our commitment to greener energy and sustainability, a key focus of Centrica’s role in Net Zero.

Our key areas of focus would be:

  • Drive engagement of NEP base
  • Increase brand advocacy and product awareness
  • Educate customers about our services and Flex Energy
  • Support KPI’s such as retention, digital adoption and cross sell

The right newsletter stands a reasonable chance of being noticed and driving brand affinity

Designing with Behavioural Science

As we defined our objectives and got a clear picture of what our customers wanted to see from customer research, it was time to move into the design stage of our email newsletter. Past versions of our newsletters had overloaded recipients with multiple equally weighted messages, so we needed a fresh approach.

Drawing from insights in Behavioural Science, the Email Design Team crafted a layout featuring one standout message, two supporting messages, and two smaller tiles, all tied together by a cohesive theme for a more organised and interconnected feel.

Incorporating valuable customer input, we also introduced a new section – a monthly “one small change” energy saving tip. Once we locked in the content style and wireframe layout, the Email Design TeamI got to work designing the email components. Our focus was on using colour to highlight important messages and employing icons instead of images for the bottom tiles to establish a clear visual hierarchy.

Applying Behavioural Science C-Factors

Saliency

  • Imagery and colour is used to draw  the eye
  • Eye tracking research reinforces  this layout is good for saliency
  • Hero article image and text layout  account for ±29%  attention in total  of eye tracking

Cognitive overload

  • Reducing overload through short content
  • Reducing overt sales messaging
  • Customers interested in energy  saving tips
  • These themes influence the design thinking

Picture superiority effect

  • Icons are used to make information easier to see and process
  • Hero article is made salient through illustrations and animations
  • Decreases the amount of information and put them into visual chunks to decrease cognitive overload

Visual hierarchy

  • Layout creates engagement via  colour,  button types and visuals
  • Introduced a monthly theme so we  can link content  together
  • Feels more connected to British Gas Energy and their relationship with the brand

Finalising the creative approach

We started off by sharing early versions of our designs with the Brand team and senior stakeholders. At first, the feedback was positive, but a recurring point was raised about our brand photography not fully aligning with the newsletter’s theme. Based on insights gained from customer research, we decided to make a bold move and transition to a purely illustration-based style for all our visuals.

This shift not only broadened our creative horizons but also allowed us to introduce engaging animations and amplify the impact of our key messages. Additionally, we identified the need to develop dedicated landing pages for our monthly newsletters to enhance the relevance of our content and better guide our readers.

After conducting customer research on our proposed newsletter approach, we received overwhelmingly positive feedback. The consensus was that the email did a great job of capturing attention, especially for those who tend to skim through their emails.
