What is our BeSci hub for?

Our decision systems

Although most of us think of ourselves as predominantly rational, thoughtful beings, psychological research consistently tells another story. Much of the information that comes into our brain is processed unconsciously in a way that is under the radar of our rational selves.

Neuroscientists believe that there are two complimentary systems driving our decisions:

  • The unconscious, impulsive, sensory-driven system also known as “Homer” or System 1
  • The rational system of thinking also known as “Mr. Spock” or System 2

Our Homer selves are at work in every decision we make, sometimes overruling Mr. Spock’s guidance on the matter. Our decisions are firmly guided by our emotions. In times of stress, when multi-tasking, or merely when unfocused, Homer’s influence over our actions is even greater.
